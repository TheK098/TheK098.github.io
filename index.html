About

Scientific and Technological Advancements in Research or S.T.A.R. Learning is a national STEM competition for students ranging from elementary to high school. To help students gain a competitive edge in their field of choice, S.T.A.R. Learning encourages academic achievement among all competitors. Our mission is to cultivate and curate ideas for socio-economic issues affecting the world today.

Prizes

We offer various prizes for the winners of each category brought to you by our valuable sponsors from numerous industries of research.

College Admissions

Participating in S.T.A.R. Learning helps students grow and develop a competitive edge in the college admissions process. 

Academics

We strive to challenge students to devise innovative solutions to global issues through extensive research and applications of STEM.

Competitions

We offer a plethora of competitions in subjects ranging from biology to computer science. These competitions have been designed to widen students' horizons and future opportunities in STEM fields. Furthermore, each category has been split into elementary, middle school, and high school divisions.

Timeline

Important dates and events for competitive events

  • July 17th

    Registration

    Register for our 2020-2021 competitions at REGFORM. Registration forms must be completed by 7/16/2020. No exceptions.

  • July 31st

    Round One Submission

    After registration, further details for events will be emailed out to participants. Students will have to complete specific tasks, and the top submissions from each event will qualify to move on to Round 2. Please note that Round One submissions must be completed on time to be eligible.

  • August 12th

    Round Two Submission

    For students who have advanced past Round One, Round Two entails creating unique, creative videos, displaying solutions to applicants' chosen problems. Further detailed instructions will be sent out by August 12, 2020 regarding submission format.

  • August 19th

    Round Three Submission

    For the competitive, driven students who have worked their way past Round Two, Round Three involves a live explanation or demonstartion of their unique ideas and solutions. In addition, a Q&A session will be conducted by distinguished judges from various fields of work. Further detailed instructions will be sent out by August 14th regarding judging criteria.

  • August 21st

    Final Results!

    The winners for each category will be released and extensively featured throughout our website and social media platforms on 8/21/20. Furthermore, they will also receive unique prizes and awards for their academically advanced, innovative solutions. Good luck!
